In 2012, a meme lasted for months. Today, the lifecycle of a meme is measured in hours.

3. Chaos cannot be Planned

The best memes are accidents. They are random, chaotic, and organic. "The Great Meme Reset" is a coordinated administrative effort.

You cannot schedule fun on a calendar. The moment you try to organize a meme revolution, it becomes homework. And the internet hates homework.
